Life in Plastic, It's Fantastic!

It’s the early 2000s, I drop my school bag and race to the TV. I’m sitting cross – legged on the floor with my eyes glued to the screen as Barbie’s latest adventure unfolds. Barbie’s sugary sweet voice told tales of friendship and adventure. Her laughter was contagious and her clothes were an array of fluffy, frilly, pink fabulous outfits!

In Barbie’s world anything and everything seemed possible. In Barbie’s world, Barbie was a musketeer, a princess, a fairy, a mermaid, an astronaut, a rockstar and so much more.

As I watch the movies, I don’t have a care in the world, my only focus is “how is barbie going to the save the day today?” In this moment, I am free, free to be anyone and anything like barbie. And once the movie ends, and the credits roll and I say bye to barbie for the day, I know I can return again to this enchanted world, where dreams know no bounds, the very next day.

The barbie movies that I cherished a kid, were more than simply entertainment they shaped my childhood.

  • Barbie Mariposa taught me the importance of believing in yourself.
  • Barbie in the 12 Dancing Princesses inspired me to find my own creative voice through art and design.
  • Barbie and Her Sisters in A Pony Tale helped me understand the idea of "If you love something, set it free."
  • Barbie and the Three Musketeers taught me to have the courage to keep working towards my dreams even if it seems impossible.
  • Barbie as Rapunzel taught me that even in the dark, there is hope.
  • Barbie and the secret door taught me the importance of facing your fears and trying new things.
  • Barbie and the Diamond Castle showed me how important friendships our in our lives.
  • Barbie in The Pink Shoes taught me to "listen to the beat of your heart and keep on dancing."
  • Barbie in a Mermaid tale taught me that something that makes you different might just be the very thing that makes you unique and can be your greatest strength.

    • Looking back at these movies, I realize that as I navigate my life here in New York City, I have to power to create my own magic and live my life to the fullest.
