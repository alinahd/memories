The Days That Shaped Me

Nostalgia is a funny thing, it creeps in quietly and when you would least expect it. Sometimes it's because of an old song I hear, or because of a picture I happen to stumble upon while going through my camera roll. Nostalgia is not always a deep ache, it is often a short reminder of what once was. It is a soft ache of knowing that different pieces of my past are still part of me but time has seperated them, leaving me with bittersweet memories. As children, we don't realize the significance of a simple afternoon but now it is those simple afternoons that come rushing back with more meaning than we ever gave them at the time. Nostalgia has a way of making ordinary moments extraodrinary, reminding me that our lives are simply a collection of small moments that have come together to shape who we our as individuals.

In the next few pages, I will be revisiting some childhood memories that have stood out to me and that I find myself reminscing about every now and then. These stories are about looking back but not with regret, instead with a deeper appreciation of the simpler times. And so, I now invite you to accompany me on this journey into my childhood.
